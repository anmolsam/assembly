// Bot lifecycle
How the bot actually joins

Zero manual steps for 100s of reps — driven entirely by the connected Google Calendar.

1

Connect calendars

Reps OAuth their Google Calendar into Recall once. Admin-wide via Workspace.

2

Detect & filter

Recall sees each Meet event. Assembly only dispatches a bot when an external attendee (non-attentive.ai) is present.

3

Auto-join & record

At start time the bot enters as "Assembly Notetaker", records audio + speaker-labeled streams.

4

Webhook out

On stream + on completion, Recall webhooks Assembly → AssemblyAI → council → HubSpot.

How we test it before 100s of reps touch it

No guessing in production. Every stage is independently verifiable.

🧪 Dry-run a single meeting

  1. Spin up a test Google Meet, invite a personal "client" Gmail (external).
  2. Confirm the bot auto-joins and starts recording.
  3. Speak scripted BANT lines; watch the live widget light up.
  4. Verify the transcript + final council verdict land on a sandbox HubSpot deal.

✅ Stage-by-stage checks

  • Audio: Recall recording downloadable & non-empty.
  • Transcript: AssemblyAI status:"completed", speakers split correctly.
  • Matching: attendee email → exactly one open deal, else parked in Airtable.
  • BANT: council confidence ≥70 before any write; never blanks an existing field.
  • HubSpot: writes to new _as fields — no clobber of AE / _ps / council data.
